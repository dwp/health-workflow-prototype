Task design for 'Book apointmnet' - May 2026 - v3

In design

Problem statement

NCATs are responsible for progressing appointment‑booking work to support HCP assessments, including booking initial appointments and rebooking (or cancelling) those that cannot go ahead. However, the current process makes it difficult for them to manage their workload efficiently. They must navigate multiple systems and rely on manual workarounds to find and progress booking work, which slows them down and increases the risk of errors.

Because there is no automated way to surface the next appropriate booking or rebooking task, NCATs face challenges such as duplicated effort, delayed prioritisation of urgent cases and unclear ownership. Rebooking adds further complexity, as NCATs must interpret why the previous appointment failed and ensure the new booking complies with policy, availability constraints and claimant needs (all of which currently require significant manual investigation).

Introducing a task-based approach, where NCATs use Get next task to receive the next booking or rebooking task from a prioritised background pool, offers an opportunity to deliver clearer workflow, better consistency and reduced cognitive load.

Task design for 'Edit report' - January 2026 - v2

Oh hold

Problem statement

After an audit outcome is shared, there is no structured or automated process for updating or editing existing reports. The current workflow relies heavily on manual handovers through phone calls, emails, and Excel trackers, which creates inefficiencies and increases the risk of errors. Health Care Professionals (HCPs) must manually locate the correct report and interpret feedback from different sources, leading to delays and inconsistencies. Additionally, Senior Health Care Professionals (SHCPs) lack visibility into the status of the ‘Edit report’ task, making it difficult to monitor progress and ensure timely completion.

To establish a structured, automated, and transparent workflow for editing reports that eliminates reliance on manual handovers, reduces errors, and improves efficiency. The solution should enable Health Care Professionals (HCPs) to easily access the correct report and apply feedback without searching through different sources, while providing Senior Health Care Professionals (SHCPs) with real-time visibility into the status of edits to ensure timely and accurate completion.

Hi-level design decisions

  • Addition of 'Edit report' task
  • Auto allocating tasks within Task landing UI
  • Use of MOJ notification badge for new auto allocated tasks
  • New auto allocated tasks highlighted in grey - taken from C-CAMS notifaction centre
  • New auto allocated tasks groupded
  • Component layout updates

Task based workflow for HCPs - August 2025 - v1

In development

Problem statement

The current process for handling cases through HAS is increasingly unsustainable as service demand grows. It relies heavily on manual workarounds, which are time-consuming, error-prone, and frustrating for users. This inefficiency not only reduces productivity but also contributes to staff dissatisfaction, affecting retention and recruitment efforts.

Evidence suggests that users may selectively process easier cases, leading to uneven workload distribution and reduced accountability. The lack of structured case ownership and personal work management tools further exacerbates this issue.

The existing system does not leverage automation, missing opportunities to streamline workflows, reduce human error, and improve overall service delivery. Automation could enable:

  • Smart case allocation based on complexity, urgency, and user expertise
  • Automated tracking and reporting, reducing administrative burden
  • Workflow standardisation, ensuring consistency and fairness
  • Enhanced user dashboards, promoting ownership and visibility of tasks
  • Time-saving integrations, allowing staff to focus on higher-value work

Without addressing these challenges, the department risks increased operational costs, reduced service quality, and ongoing difficulties in maintaining a skilled and motivated workforce.

Hi-level design decisions

  • Integration of allocting a task and 'open tasks'
  • Table updates to include 'Due in' to give users an idea of priority
  • Notification if a new task has come in for a case previously worked on
  • Option to mark multiple tasks as complete
  • Confirmation for marking tasks as complete

Appointee details - April 2025 - vxx

Live

Problem statement

If someone claiming PIP or WCA is incapable of managing their own affairs due to mental incapacity or severe physical disability, then an appointee may be assigned. The appointee will act on behalf of the claimant for benefit purposes.

An appointee can be an individual, or an organisation.

However, there have been instances where the claimant has been contacted rather than the appointee.

Agents need to access various systems using manual processes in order to find out if an appointee has been assigned to a referral.

This is time consuming for the agent. It opens up for error and risks contact with the incorrect person.

This costs the department money and causes lack of trust

Hi-level design decisions

  • Addition of 'Appointee' section into 'Claimant details'
  • Addition of 'Appointee' into side nav
  • Use of 'Warning text' in 'Personal details' when appointee is registered
  • Appointee details are displayed to agent in 'Appointee' section
  • 'No appointee' message added to 'Appointee' section when no appointee is registered
